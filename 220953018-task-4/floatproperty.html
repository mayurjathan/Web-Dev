Float Example

A float (short for "floating-point number") is a data type used in programming to represent real numbers that have a fractional component. Floats are particularly useful for representing values that require a high degree of precision, such as measurements, scientific calculations, or financial data.

When working with floats, be mindful of potential precision issues, especially when comparing two float values. It's often safer to check if they are "close enough" rather than equal, using a small threshold (epsilon).
